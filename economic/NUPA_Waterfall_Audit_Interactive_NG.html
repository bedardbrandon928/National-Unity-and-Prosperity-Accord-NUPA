Technical note — the 20% compounding mechanism: The 20% annual rent growth is not a projection or assumption. It is mechanically guaranteed by the framework's closed reinvestment loop. Twenty percent of every naira collected in ground rent is contractually hard-coded back into hub infrastructure — power, roads, maintenance, and skills modules. This continuous infrastructure investment increases the productive capacity and utility value of each hub, which justifies the corresponding rent increase the following year. The tenant's profit margin grows in parallel because the infrastructure they operate on improves every year, meaning they are not paying more for the same asset — they are paying more for a demonstrably better one. The governor's state treasury receives a larger absolute payment each year because the underlying asset is being continuously upgraded at zero cost to the government, the state, or the community. The 20% growth rate and the 20% reinvestment fund are the same number because they are the same mechanism: a self-funding, self-appreciating wealth engine with no external fuel source required.

The 7 tax streams

1. VAT (7.5%) — on all commercial activity within hubs. Digital invoicing makes this the most reliable stream.
2. Corporate Income Tax (30%) — on profits of Tier-1 data centres, solar farms, and manufacturing plants.
3. Payroll Tax / PIT (25%) — on high-value human wages protected by the AI Firewall.
4. Capital Gains Tax (30%) — on appreciation of industrial assets and shared transfers within hubs.
5. Development Levy (4%) — of assessable profits from all hub participants, for secondary regional infrastructure.
6. Stamp Duties — collected on 99-year bankable ground leases and high-volume commercial contracts.
7. Secondary Industry Growth — taxes on security, logistics, and catering firms that emerge to service the hubs.
