# NUPALTA 

The National Unity and Prosperity Land Trust Authority (NUPALTA) is a non-federal, self-dissolving facilitator agency designed to bridge the transition from the current legacy economic system to the NUPA architectural operating system. Its primary responsibility is to handle the technical, legal, and operational "bootstrapping" of industrial hubs without creating a permanent federal bureaucracy.

### Phase 1: Ignition and Operational Setup
During the initial "Phase 1 Ignition," NUPALTA is responsible for the preliminary actions required to activate the framework:
*   **Sovereign Opt-In Management:** NUPALTA serves as the sole independent agency for handling Sovereign Opt-Ins. Crucially, to respect Tribal autonomy and prevent federal paternalism, the agency is legally barred from initiating contact with Tribal Nations; it can only respond to a voluntary execution of a Memorandum of Sovereign Understanding (MOSU) or Interjurisdictional Sovereignty Compact (ISC).
*   **Land Identification and Scouting:** The agency identifies specific ancestral-adjacent Bureau of Land Management (BLM) acreage for hub activation. It employs site scouts and surveyors to verify the viability of these parcels for Tier-1 industrial use, such as data centers or solar farms.
*   **Corporate Outreach:** Working with designated Tier-1 law firms, NUPALTA issues the "Notice of Strategic Opportunity" to global C-suite executives. It manages the neutral escrow accounts where private corporate reservation deposits are held to fund the framework's start-up costs.

### Verification and Verification Assistance Program (VAP)
A core responsibility of NUPALTA is managing the **Justice Engine**, which involves the forensic settlement of historical debts.
*   **Digital Submission Portal:** NUPALTA launches and maintains the digital portal for the Verification Assistance Program (VAP).
*   **Forensic Lineage Verification:** The agency facilitates Phase 2 of the VAP, conducting race-blind forensic genealogical surveys to identify verified 1865 lineage descendants. This process is funded privately by the 1% Framework Usage Fee to ensure zero cost to taxpayers.

### Governance Transition and CLT Formation
NUPALTA acts as the architectural bridge that moves land from federal mismanagement into localized stewardship:
*   **Board Chartering:** The agency is responsible for the legal chartering and seating of the Variable Quad-Partite Vetting (VQPV) boards.
*   **Licensing Transfer:** Upon Congressional authorization, NUPALTA facilitates the transfer of the Federal Asset Sovereignty License (FASL) to the individual Community Land Trusts (CLTs).
*   **Operational Oversight:** NUPALTA manages the framework's operational integrity until local governance is fully established.

### Mandatory Dissolution and Legacy Roles
By design, NUPALTA is a temporary entity intended to prevent the "Policy Somersaults" associated with permanent government agencies.
*   **The Sunset Event:** The agency is mandated to formally dissolve once approximately 3,000 VQPV boards are seated across the 24 million acres of authorized land.
*   **The Legacy Administrator:** After dissolution, NUPALTA leaves behind a single "Legacy Administrator". This role is strictly limited to acting as a tie-breaker for local board gridlock and ensuring ongoing adherence to the human-labor protection and fiduciary safety mandates.

### International Adaptations (NUPALTA-NG and NUPALTA-LR)
In international theaters like Nigeria (NUPALTA-NG) and Liberia (NUPALTA-LR), the agency’s responsibilities are adapted to local "licensing hardware". These responsibilities include utilizing the Nigerian Land Use Act of 1978 or the Liberian Land Rights Act of 2018 to formalize customary land ownership into irrevocable private-sector licenses. Similar to the U.S. model, these international facilitator agencies sunset automatically once local community boards are seated.
